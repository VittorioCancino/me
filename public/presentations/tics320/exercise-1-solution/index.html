What to watch for

Three things students usually miss

❌
Merging Ticket and Purchase into one entity

They represent different actions — buying entry vs. buying drinks. One is tied to an event slot, the other is a bar transaction. Merging them creates an insert anomaly immediately.

🧩
Missing the PurchaseItem bridge

A purchase can include multiple drinks, and the same drink can appear in many purchases. That's M:N — you cannot model it without a bridge table. PurchaseItem is where the quantity lives, not in Purchase or Drink.

✨
Not linking Purchase to Event

The exercise doesn't say it explicitly — but ask yourself: "Can I report bar revenue per event night?" Without Purchase → Event, no. This is a good example of designing for the questions you'll need to answer, not just the facts you have.

Reasoning out loud

Walk it like this

1. Start with obvious entities

"Customer, Event, Drink… these clearly exist on their own."

2. Identify actions

"Buying a ticket" → Ticket. "Buying drinks" → Purchase. Actions become entities too.

3. Fix complexity

"A purchase has multiple drinks" → M:N → we need a bridge. PurchaseItem is born.

🎯 Notice how every piece represents something real. If you can explain it in words, you can model it.
