University Enrollment Management System

Case Study: University Enrollment Management System

A university is currently managing its course enrollment process manually using spreadsheets and paper forms. This has led to frequent errors, duplicated records, and difficulty tracking which students are enrolled in which courses.

The administration has decided to develop a system that allows them to organize and manage this information in a structured way.

Each semester, students register for one or more courses offered by the university. Every course is assigned to a professor who is responsible for teaching it. Professors may teach multiple courses during the same semester.

Case Study · 2 of 2

University Enrollment Management System

The university needs to keep track of which students are enrolled in each course. Additionally, for each enrollment, the university wants to store relevant information about the student's performance in that course.

The system should also allow administrators to easily identify:

  • Which courses a student is currently enrolled in
  • Which students are enrolled in a given course
  • Which professor is responsible for each course

Your task is to analyze this situation and design a conceptual model that accurately represents the information and relationships involved.

Clinic Appointment Management System

Case Study: Clinic Appointment Management System

A private clinic is currently managing its daily operations using paper forms and spreadsheets. This has caused frequent problems when organizing appointments, assigning doctors, and keeping track of the patients who attend the clinic.

In many cases, the staff must manually search through records to verify which doctor will see a patient, what specialty that doctor belongs to, and in which consultation room the appointment will take place.

To improve its organization, the clinic wants to develop a system that stores information about its patients, doctors, medical specialties, consultation rooms, and appointments.

Case Study · Level 2 · 2 of 3

Clinic Appointment Management System

Patients may visit the clinic many times over time, and doctors may attend many different patients during their work schedule. Each appointment is scheduled for a specific date and time, is associated with one patient, and is attended by one doctor.

The clinic also wants to keep track of the specialty of each doctor, since each doctor works in a particular medical area such as cardiology, pediatrics, or dermatology. In addition, every appointment must be assigned to a consultation room, because the clinic has several rooms available throughout the day and needs to know where each consultation will occur.

Case Study · Level 2 · 3 of 3

Clinic Appointment Management System

The system should allow the clinic to:

  • identify the appointments scheduled for each patient
  • determine which doctor will attend each appointment
  • know the medical specialty associated with each doctor
  • identify the consultation room assigned to each appointment

Your task is to analyze this situation and design a conceptual model that accurately represents the information and relationships involved.

Event Management System

Case Study: Event Management System

A company that organizes professional conferences and academic events is currently managing its operations using spreadsheets, email threads, and manually updated documents. As the number of events has grown, this process has become difficult to maintain, especially when tracking attendees, assigning speakers to sessions, and organizing the rooms where activities take place.

To improve coordination, the company wants to develop a system that stores information about the events it organizes, the sessions that belong to each event, the speakers who participate in those sessions, the attendees who register for the events, and the rooms where the sessions are held.

Case Study · Level 3 · 2 of 3

Event Management System

Each event may include several sessions scheduled throughout the day. Every session belongs to a single event, has a title, a scheduled date and time, and takes place in one room. A room may be used for many sessions over time.

Speakers may participate in different sessions, and a session may include more than one speaker. The organization also wants to keep track of the role each speaker has in a session, such as main speaker, panelist, or moderator.

Attendees may register for different events over time, and each event may have many registered attendees. For every registration, the company wants to store the date on which the attendee registered and the current registration status.

Case Study · Level 3 · 3 of 3

Event Management System

The system should allow the organization to:

  • identify the sessions that belong to each event
  • determine which room is assigned to each session
  • know which speakers participate in each session
  • store the role of each speaker in a given session
  • identify which attendees are registered for each event
  • store the registration date and status for each attendee registration

Your task is to analyze this situation and design a conceptual model that accurately represents the information and relationships involved.

Vehicle Dealership Network Management System

Case Study: Vehicle Dealership Network Management System

A company operates a network of vehicle dealerships in different cities and countries. Until now, each dealership has managed its information independently using spreadsheets, local databases, and manual documents. As the business has expanded, this has made it difficult to maintain consistent records about vehicles, branches, sellers, buyers, and the operations carried out at each location.

The company now wants to develop a centralized system that stores information about its dealerships, the branches that belong to each dealership, the cities and countries where those branches are located, the vehicles managed by the company, and the users who interact with the platform.

Case Study · Level 4 · 2 of 4

Vehicle Dealership Network Management System

The company works with different kinds of users. All users share common information such as an identifier, name, email address, and phone number. However, some users act as buyers, while others act as sellers. Buyers are the people or organizations that purchase or rent vehicles from the company. Sellers are the staff members or representatives responsible for publishing, managing, and negotiating the available vehicles in the system.

The company also manages different kinds of vehicles. Every vehicle has common information such as an identifier, brand, model, manufacturing year, and base price. However, depending on the type of vehicle, the company needs to store additional specific information.

Case Study · Level 4 · 3 of 4

Vehicle Dealership Network Management System

Each dealership may operate several branches. A branch is identified within the context of its dealership and has information such as its branch number, address, and contact phone. Every branch is located in one city, and every city belongs to one country. The company needs to know in which branch each vehicle is currently available.

The business supports both vehicle purchases and vehicle rentals. Buyers may purchase vehicles that are available through the company, and the system must store the date and final amount of each purchase. Buyers may also rent vehicles for a period of time, and for each rental the company needs to record the start date, end date, and agreed rental price.

Case Study · Level 4 · 4 of 4

Vehicle Dealership Network Management System

The system should allow the company to:

  • identify the branches that belong to each dealership
  • determine in which city and country each branch is located
  • know which vehicles are available in each branch
  • distinguish the different kinds of vehicles and store their specific information
  • distinguish between buyers and sellers while preserving the common information they share
  • record which buyer purchased a vehicle and the details of that purchase
  • record which buyer rented a vehicle and the details of that rental

Your task is to analyze this situation and design a conceptual model that accurately represents the information and relationships involved.

Corporate Project and Workforce Management System

Case Study: Corporate Project and Workforce Management System

A technology consulting company is currently coordinating its internal operations through spreadsheets, email threads, and disconnected departmental records. This approach has become increasingly difficult to manage as the company has grown, especially when trying to track employees, assign people to projects, understand reporting structures, and coordinate project planning across multiple departments.

To improve control over its operations, the company wants to develop a centralized system that stores information about its employees, departments, and projects. Every employee belongs to a department, and each department is responsible for overseeing the projects under its area.

Case Study · Level 5 · 2 of 4

Corporate Project and Workforce Management System

Not all employees have the same responsibilities within the organization. Although all employees share common information such as identification, contact details, and hiring information, some employees work in management roles, some are responsible for leading work teams, and others work as specialists in particular technical areas. The company wants the system to reflect both the information shared by all employees and the information that is specific to these different roles.

In addition to storing personnel information, the company needs to represent the internal reporting structure among employees. In practice, some employees supervise the work of others, while some employees are supervised by someone else.

Case Study · Level 5 · 3 of 4

Corporate Project and Workforce Management System

The company also wants to keep track of project participation. Employees may work on different projects over time, and each project may involve several employees with different responsibilities. For each assignment, the company needs to know how many hours have been assigned and the role that the employee performs within that project.

Project planning has also become more complex because some projects cannot begin or advance until other projects have reached certain milestones. In other words, some projects depend on the progress or completion of other projects.

Case Study · Level 5 · 4 of 4

Corporate Project and Workforce Management System

The system should allow the company to:

  • identify the employees that belong to each department
  • determine which projects are overseen by each department
  • distinguish different categories of employees while preserving the information they share
  • represent which employees supervise other employees
  • identify which employees work on each project
  • store assignment information such as hours and role within a project
  • represent project dependencies when one project relies on another

Your task is to analyze this situation and design a conceptual model that accurately represents the information and relationships involved.
